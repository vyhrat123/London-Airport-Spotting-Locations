LHR

London Heathrow

Europe's busiest airport with two parallel runways that switch direction at 3pm daily. Expect BA and Virgin heavies, A380s galore, and traffic from every corner of the globe. Getting around is easy — the Tube serves the airport directly.

Two runways A380 hub Tube access Runway switch 3pm
01
Myrtle Avenue
27L arrivals Public land Free
The most iconic spotting location in the UK. A short residential road ending in grassland just off the Hatton Cross tube station, sitting directly under the threshold of runway 27L. Aircraft pass as low as 40 feet overhead — genuinely jaw-dropping.
Tip: Best light is afternoon. Bring a wide-angle lens for dramatic undercarriage shots. Gets crowded at weekends.
02
Terminal 5 Car Park (Level 5)
27R departures T5 car park Paid parking
From the top level of the T5 short-stay car park you get excellent elevated views of aircraft taxiing and lining up for departure on Runway 27R. A great option when 27L spotting at Myrtle is less productive.
Tip: Stay the minimum time to keep costs down. Elevated position means better telephoto angles.
03
Eastern Perimeter Road
27R arrivals Public road Free
When aircraft arrive on 27R and Myrtle Avenue is out of play, walking along the Eastern Perimeter Road (starting from Hatton Cross station) offers alternative positions along the fence. Follow other spotters — they know where to stop.
Tip: Security regularly patrols and may ask you to step back a metre from the fence — they won't remove you from public land.
04
Harmondsworth Moor
09L/09R departures Country park Free
A peaceful country park to the west of the airport offering a unique perspective on aircraft heading east. Good for long-lens photography and video when the runways are in easterly mode. A pleasant walk is involved.
Tip: Best from late morning to afternoon. Wear comfortable shoes — there's a good walk to reach the prime viewing area.
05
Marriott Hotel Rooftop
Northern runway Hotel guests Spotter packages
The Heathrow Marriott sits north of the airport and offers a superb elevated rooftop vantage point over the northern runway (27L/09R). The hotel offers dedicated spotters' packages through its website — pricey but unique.
Tip: Book via the hotel website and specifically request the spotter package for rooftop access and runway-facing room.
06
Northern Perimeter Road
Both runways Public road Free
Walking sections of the Northern Perimeter Road gives you different angles across both runways depending on the active configuration. A good backup when specific spots aren't working due to the day's runway direction.
Tip: Use Flightradar24 to know in advance which runway is active before deciding where to walk.
LGW

London Gatwick

The world's busiest single-runway airport, 30 miles south of London. Strong on low-cost carriers — easyJet dominates — alongside BA, TUI, and unusual visitors like Norse Atlantic and Westjet. The single runway keeps the action focused in one place.

Single runway easyJet hub LCC variety Two terminals
01
Lowfield Heath Recreation Ground
26L touchdowns Public park Free
The best spot for touchdown shots on runway 26L. Loads of space to spread out and you'll often find familiar faces from the community here. One of the most reliable and comfortable spots at Gatwick with open views.
Tip: Check the active runway direction before travelling — this spot works best when 26L is in use for arrivals.
02
Blue Car Park 1 (Level 5)
26L short finals South Terminal £13+ for 2hrs Signs discourage lingering
The top level of Blue Multi-Storey Car Park 1 gives good elevated views of arrivals on short final approach to 26L. Note that signage indicates spotters aren't encouraged to loiter — keep it brief and move on.
Tip: You face into the sun from this position — best in overcast conditions or early morning.
03
South Terminal Crash Gate Area
Runway 08 departures South-east perimeter Free
The most popular spotting point at Gatwick for taxiing and departure shots when runway 08 is active (roughly 30% of the time). Multiple angles available along the fence — a ladder helps for cleaner shots over the top.
Tip: Do NOT park near the crash gate or under the flightpath — security will move your car. Park further along by the Flight pub and walk back (~20 mins).
04
Gatwick Railway Station Platforms
South Terminal apron Platform ticket Low cost
The airport's own railway station linked to the South Terminal offers surprisingly decent views of the apron and some aircraft movements — a handy option if you're arriving by Gatwick Express and want a quick look without going further afield.
Tip: Useful for logging registrations rather than photography. Combine with a brief terminal visit.
05
Gatwick Aviation Museum
Western runway end Fri–Sun only Entry fee
Located on the perimeter road with two fields and an observation deck looking into the western end of the runway. Views are through security fencing but largely unobstructed — mostly departures climbing out. Open Friday to Sunday only.
Tip: Worth combining with a look at the museum's collection of preserved aircraft. Check opening hours before visiting.
06
BLOC Hotel (South Terminal)
Full airport panorama Hotel guests Room rates
Situated atop the South Terminal, rooms facing airside give sweeping views of both terminals and sections of the runway — a unique overnight option for the committed spotter. Depending on room allocation you may see most aircraft movements.
Tip: Request an airside-facing room when booking. Upper floors give the best unobstructed views.
LCY

London City Airport

A unique airport carved into the Royal Docks, hemmed in by Canary Wharf's towers. Its short runway and steep 5.5° approach angle mean only specially certified aircraft can operate here — mostly regional jets. Spectacular urban backdrop for photography.

Steep approach 5.5° Docklands setting DLR access Regional jets
01
Royal Albert Dock Footpath
Full runway & approaches Public path Free
The prime spot at London City. Walk the footpath along the Royal Albert Dock and watch Embraer jets bank dramatically over Canary Wharf's towers on their steep final approach. The combination of glass skyscrapers and low aircraft is unlike anywhere else in the world.
Tip: DLR to Royal Albert station puts you right there. Golden hour light over the dock is spectacular in summer.
02
Fox @ Connaught Pub
Riverside approach views Public — buy a drink Menu prices
A riverside terrace pub just across the water from the airport that's become a genuine spotting institution. You get views of the approach spectacle without the full roar of jet engines. Popular with after-work City workers and aviation vloggers alike.
Tip: The terrace is best in summer. Arrive early on busy evenings to grab a table with a direct sightline to the runway.
03
Yi-Ban Restaurant
Across the water Restaurant — reservation advised £26–40 per head
For a thoroughly civilised spotting session, this restaurant sits directly opposite the airport across the dock. Views from outside tables are excellent, particularly in summer evenings. Good for a longer stay with food and planes.
Tip: Views from outside tables only — inside you're blocked by glass. Best in summer. Ample parking on site.
STN

London Stansted

Thirty miles north-east of London, Stansted is the low-cost carrier capital — Ryanair's biggest UK base. Cargo traffic is a highlight, with Cargolux, FedEx, UPS, and various Asian carriers making regular appearances. Single runway, sprawling aprons.

Ryanair hub Cargo variety Single runway Executive jets
01
Burton End Village
Opposite side of runway Public road & fields Free
The go-to spot for Stansted — a tiny village on the opposite side of the runway to the terminal. Head off the M11, past Molehill Green and Elsenham, follow signs past the fields to reach approach shot positions at the end of the road.
Tip: Park at the road end and walk into the field for clean approach shots. You won't be bothered here. Good spot for a relaxed, unpressured session.
02
Belmer Road
Runway & NW aprons Public road Free Police may occasionally ask questions
Probably the best runway views at Stansted — Belmer Road runs to the north-west where aircraft arrive, depart and taxi in front of you. The spot is just off the road behind a row of trees. Airport security has no authority here as it's public land.
Tip: Police have been known to ask questions but have no grounds to remove you from public land. Stay calm and confident.
03
Great Hallingbury Footpath
04 approach shots Public footpath Free Spotters discouraged
Park near the church at Great Hallingbury, walk through the garden and follow the public footpath into an open field. Good for approach shots on runway 04. The airport actively discourages spotters from this location, though the footpath itself is public.
Tip: Discretion is advised here. Keep a low profile and be respectful of the local area.
04
Birchanger Services (M11)
Runway 05 end Private land — be discreet Free (with purchase) Private land
The Birchanger motorway services close to the end of runway 05 give views of aircraft close to rotation. This is private land so keep things discreet, buy a coffee, and be prepared to move on if security asks.
Tip: Good for a quick pop-in between other spots rather than a long session. Check Flightradar24 to know which end is active.
LTN

London Luton

Thirty miles north of London off the M1. Luton is the place for business jets — dozens of Gulfstreams, Falcons, and Global Expresses pass through daily alongside easyJet, Ryanair, Wizz Air, and TUI. Authorities are generally relaxed about photography on public land.

Bizjet paradise easyJet & Wizz Photography friendly Single runway
01
Taxiway Fire Station Area
All taxiway traffic Near Mid Stay car park 15 mins free
All traffic on the taxiway passes this point in both directions, giving you different angles depending on movement direction. Located near the Mid Stay car park. First 15 minutes of parking is free — ideal for a quick reconnaissance.
Tip: Up to 30 minutes costs £4. Good for logging registrations quickly before moving to other spots.
02
Car Park Overview (Signature FBO)
Runway, South Apron, Taxiways Airport car park Paid
Good panoramic views across the runway, South Apron, Taxiways Delta and Foxtrot, the Signature FBO, Gulfstream hangar, and main apron — all from the elevated car park. A great all-rounder for logging business jets and commercial traffic simultaneously.
Tip: Luton is the top London airport for bizjets — you may well see a Global 7500 or a Bombardier Challenger here that won't appear at the other airports.
03
Dane Street
Eastern runway section Public road Free parking
A side street off Chiltern Green Road that overlooks the eastern section of the runway. Free parking and a quiet, low-pressure environment — ideal if you want to avoid paying for airport car parks while still getting decent runway views.
Tip: Best for easterly operations. Combine with the car park area for a full circuit of the airport's spotting spots.
04
Someries Castle & Surroundings
Runway approaches Public footpath / heritage site Free
The area around the historic Someries Castle ruins to the south-east of the airport provides elevated ground with open views of runway approaches. A more scenic backdrop than most spotting locations — medieval ruins under a busy flightpath.
Tip: A pleasant walk from surrounding roads. Good for a relaxed half-day out combining aviation with a slice of Bedfordshire history.
05
Hotel Runway View
Runway panorama Hotel guests Room rates
The hotel adjacent to the airport offers rooms overlooking the runway — email ahead and request a runway-facing room and the hotel will do its best to accommodate. The view from the room (and the road outside) gives solid photography opportunities without needing to move far.
Tip: Email rather than call — put "runway view room request" clearly in your message so it gets to the right person.
